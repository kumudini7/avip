Accounts Payable Invoice Processing Workflow
Purpose

This document describes the current-state invoice processing workflow for Acme Manufacturing. The objective is to understand manual activities, identify bottlenecks, and evaluate opportunities for intelligent automation.

Business Overview

Acme Manufacturing receives approximately 2,500 supplier invoices per month from more than 180 vendors.

Invoices arrive through multiple channels:

Email (70%)
Paper Mail (15%)
Vendor Portal (10%)
EDI (5%)

The AP team consists of:

1 AP Manager
4 AP Specialists
2 Finance Approvers

Average invoice value: $4,800

Current Workflow
Step 1 – Invoice Receipt

Owner: AP Specialist

Activities:

Monitor AP mailbox
Download invoice attachments
Scan paper invoices
Save invoices to shared folder

Average Time:
5 minutes per invoice

Pain Points:

Multiple input channels
Manual downloading
Missing attachments
Step 2 – Invoice Registration

Owner:
AP Specialist

Activities:

Open ERP system
Create invoice record
Enter:
Vendor Name
Invoice Number
Invoice Date
Due Date
Amount
Tax

Average Time:
8 minutes

Pain Points

Manual typing
Duplicate invoices
Data entry errors
Step 3 – PO Matching

Owner:
AP Specialist

Activities

Search Purchase Order
Compare
Vendor
Quantity
Price
Tax

Decision

Match Found?

YES → Continue

NO → Exception Queue

Average Time

6 minutes

Pain Points

Missing PO
Price mismatch
Incorrect quantities
Step 4 – Approval

Owner:
Department Manager

Activities

Review invoice
Verify budget
Approve or Reject

Average Time

1–3 business days

Pain Points

Delayed approvals
Email reminders
Lost emails
Step 5 – ERP Posting

Owner:
Finance Team

Activities

Post approved invoice
Validate GL coding
Schedule payment

Average Time

4 minutes

Pain Points

Incorrect GL codes
Manual validation
Step 6 – Payment

Owner:
Finance

Activities

Payment batch generation
Bank file creation
Payment confirmation

Average Time

Daily payment run

Step 7 – Archive

Owner:
AP Specialist

Activities

Save invoice PDF
Store approval history
Update document repository

Pain Points

Manual filing
Difficult document search

Conclusion

The current invoice process relies heavily on manual data entry, email-based approvals, and repetitive validation activities. Implementing intelligent invoice automation with OCR, workflow automation, ERP integration, and AI-assisted validation is expected to significantly improve efficiency, reduce operational costs, and increase processing accuracy.
